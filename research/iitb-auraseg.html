AURASeg: keeping urban roads drivable

IIT Bombay · Drivable area segmentation for Indian roads

I built AURASeg—Attention-guided Upsampling with Residual boundary refinements—to map drivable space on chaotic Indian roads. The model outperformed YOLOP on mIoU and F1, but the real win was how it held the lane under rain, glare, and reflective puddles.

Problem space. Dense, reflective roads are brutal on standard segmentation baselines. Puddles, tar patches, and headlight glare all masquerade as “safe” space and pull controllers into danger zones.

Solution. AURASeg couples attention-guided upsampling with residual boundary refinements so the decoder tracks both texture and geometry. A lightweight auxiliary edge detector feeds the decoder to keep curbs crisp while staying real-time.

Moments. Hitting better mIoU and F1 than YOLOP was satisfying, but the real moment was a wet-night sequence where AURASeg kept the drivable ribbon tight instead of drifting into oncoming headlights.

Deployment. We pruned and quantised the network for a Jetson Xavier, then wrapped it in a ROS 2 node that streams masks with a 12 ms budget. On-board latency stayed flat even with additional diagnostics switched on.

Outlook. Next up is domain-adaptive fine-tuning so fog, dust, and rain stop feeling like edge cases. The goal is to keep the model honest when the scenery shifts.

Robust perception is the quiet confidence behind autonomous navigation.
